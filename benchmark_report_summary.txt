Commands used
-------------

Activate environment:

  cd E:\Document\ML\CRATE-LM\crate-lm
  .\.venv\Scripts\Activate.ps1

Install dependencies:

  pip install torch tiktoken datasets tqdm requests

Prepare data and train the full small benchmark:

  .\run_benchmark_small.ps1

Alternative separated commands:

  .\run_benchmark_small.ps1 -PrepareOnly
  .\run_benchmark_small.ps1 -TrainOnly

Create fine-tuning checkpoint directories from the best Shakespeare checkpoint:

  New-Item -ItemType Directory -Force out-crate-small-ft-wikitext2
  Copy-Item out-crate-small-shakespeare\ckpt-400.pt out-crate-small-ft-wikitext2\ckpt.pt -Force

  Copy-Item out-crate-small-shakespeare\ckpt-400.pt out-crate-small-ft-tinystories\ckpt.pt -Force

Fine-tune on Wikitext-2:

  python train.py config/finetune_wikitext2_small.py `
    --out_dir=out-crate-small-ft-wikitext2 `
    --device=cuda `
    --max_iters=1400 `
    --always_save_checkpoint=True

Fine-tune on TinyStories:

  python train.py config/finetune_tinystories_small.py `
    --out_dir=out-crate-small-ft-tinystories `
    --device=cuda `
    --max_iters=1400 `
    --always_save_checkpoint=True

Evaluate Wikitext-2 fine-tuned checkpoint:

  python eval_checkpoint.py `
    --out_dir=out-crate-small-ft-wikitext2 `
    --dataset=wikitext2 `
    --device=cuda `
    --eval_iters=50

Evaluate TinyStories fine-tuned checkpoint:

  python eval_checkpoint.py `
    --out_dir=out-crate-small-ft-tinystories `
    --dataset=tinystories `
    --device=cuda `
    --eval_iters=50

Interpretation
--------------

CRATE-small was successfully trained from scratch on the source/tested dataset
and on two novel datasets. The model was also fine-tuned from a Shakespeare
checkpoint and evaluated on the two novel datasets.

In this setup, fine-tuning from the Shakespeare checkpoint underperformed
training from scratch on both Wikitext-2 and TinyStories. This suggests that the
Shakespeare checkpoint did not transfer well to these novel datasets under the
current fine-tuning configuration. A likely reason is domain mismatch:
Shakespeare is small and stylistically different from Wikipedia-style text
and TinyStories.


Connection to the CRATE-LM paper
--------------------------------

The CRATE-LM paper proposes a white-box language model architecture designed to
improve neuron-level interpretability. The main paper focuses on interpretability
analysis and neuron-level explanation quality.

This mini benchmark does not reproduce the full neuron-level interpretability
evaluation from the paper. Instead, it verifies the training and evaluation
pipeline for CRATE-small and compares two training settings:

  1. Training from scratch on novel datasets.
  2. Fine-tuning from a Shakespeare-trained checkpoint on novel datasets.

The experiment shows that CRATE-small can be trained and evaluated successfully,
but in this small-scale setting, scratch training performs better than
fine-tuning from Shakespeare on both novel datasets.
